I am Shanthanu, an Analyst & Engineer trying to change the way we interact with the world around us.

Through my efforts in building solutions towards everyday challenges, I fell in love with data analytics. This website intends to serve as a record of my journey, as a guide perhaps, for others who might choose explore this realm, as well as a collection of my own thoughts around data analytics and science.

How it all began

Millions of years ago, back in 2009, I developed an Automated Lighting Control System. I've always been passionate about energy conservation and automation.

A simple design, this system contained 2 LDRs and a Gate Logic with a 7400 series IC right at the entrance of a door to detect when a person enters or exits a room, connected to an Adder circuit to keep track of how many people are in the room. Room goes empty, lights go off.

Designing this system was probably my first analytical exercise:
Average Power Consumption in facilities to justify the cost
Average Walking Speed of a person to set Capacitor release times
Average Dimensions of a person to space the LDRs... and then some.

Watching it all come together in perfect harmony is a moment I'll never forget.

Epiphany

Having experienced a high from designing energy efficient systems, I got into construction. I wanted to redefine everything we knew about building management systems.

During this time, I found myself analyzing design operations and automating the process as most of it was repetitive. Being an Excel Wiz, I created some reusable templates that could be used for any brand in any system saving a truck load of time.

Once again, I found myself straying away from the design activity and spending more time analyzing various processes and automating them.

And that's when it hit me: It wasn't necessarily the Systems Design or Energy Conservation that drove me. It was the opportunity to Analyze and Improve upon that released my dopamine.

The Anarisuto

This was when I realized what the practical application of Statistics & Probability was, for the first time in my life probably.
A few years later, whilst enrolled in a Masters program, I tried building a lost & found app. I had to come to terms with the reality that my skills in Excel, however advanced, no longer sufficed to perform the analytics I wished to with the scale of data I now dealt with.

I had to evolve

And so, in an attempt to build the app and everything around it myself, I mastered Python, the Django MVC framework, SQL, a variety of Data Analytics principles, and gained a nice view into the magic that is Data Science.

My thoughts scattered around this website should (I hope) give anyone who chances down the same path as I at least half a leg up.

Oh, and that last image right up here? Yeah, that's made purely on Google Sheets. You play with Data Visualization long enough and you'll find yourself painting masterpieces with data.
